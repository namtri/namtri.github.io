3D Prints and Designs

I've been developing tools and other objects to solve problems around the house. The program I've been using for design is OpenSCAD for it's simple interface and extensive library of plugins.

I never liked the OEM visors on later E-Types. They always obstructed my visibility of traffic lights and the material had broken down and wasn't very attactive. I decided to try and make my own visors using acrylic or polycarbonate, but needed a bracket to attach the visor to the mounting brackets.

What is next for this project? Well, I do need to make some more changes as the visors aren't stable, so I may need to add an inner recess for an o-ring or a piece of rubber.

In 2024 I had started a project of updating the interior of my RX-7, one of those projects being replacing the shifter boot and emergency boot. When I pulled the interior apart, I found that the bracket that the leather boot attached to was completely destroyed. I was able to take some basic measurements off the old piece and worked up a replacement in CAD.

Along with the emergency brake trim piece, I found I wanted to finish the shifter boot as well. The main problem was the openning was large and had a little slop between the shifter and the boot. I came up with this shifter collar. It's contoured to match the bottom of my shift knob and snuggly fits the boot opening.

During the restoration of the interior, my mirror control switch had a broken tab (a common problem with 90's Japanese plastics) and took some inspiration from another maker and fashioned my own switch strap and ring to retain the control in the door panel.

This is an organizer I designed to hold the set of projector cards.

I have an odd shaped sink and the faucet pointed straight down which didn't quite reach my working area, so I designed this adapter to direct the water in the direction of the sink.

This was a project to organize my cartridges and batteries for my vape. It allows for multiple sized batteries and 4 additional 510 threaded cartridges. Also contains a provision for a poker tool.

This project was started as a way to make it easier for my dad to refill his Vapor Genie due to his Parkinsons. It's a bat shaped vaporizer with a breech bowl. It rolls very easily if not held and as he loses motor control I could see it becoming more difficult to refill.
Enter the Fill-a-Bowl. A two piece system which includes a base to support the vaporizer, and a funnel to make it easier to refill. The base works well as just a pipe stand.

As I've continued to explore the BOSL library, I wanted to learn about joiners, beziers, and masks. This project utilizes those features of the library to design a modular and extendable cable clip retainer.

I wanted to design a modular, extendable cable retainer which could prevent one or more USB-C cables from falling to the floor. The idea here is that each block and be joined to a neighbor to support multible cables.

Then I decided that I could split the model right down the middle to create, left and right end-caps.
