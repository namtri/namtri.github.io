About Me

I'm a software engineer with 20+ years of experience building things — professionally in PHP, JavaScript, and the LAMP stack, and personally in whatever rabbit hole I've fallen into that week. My career has taken me from telecom APIs and CRM systems to healthcare data conversions and interactive TV platforms, which is a roundabout way of saying I'm comfortable with a wide range of problems and even more comfortable Googling the ones I'm not.

Outside of the day job, I own two cars that represent two very different relationships with ambition. The RX-7 was the car of my generation — the one you drove into the ground in Gran Turismo and swore you'd own someday. It turns out that meeting your heroes isn't always all it's cracked up to be, and keeping it alive has pushed me well outside my comfort zone more times than I'd like to admit. I wouldn't have it any other way. The 1968 Jaguar E-Type is a different kind of commitment: a car I inherited from my dad that I know deserves a full restoration, and which I'm approaching with the careful procrastination of someone who understands exactly how deep that rabbit hole goes.

When I can't find a part I need — a replacement bracket, a custom visor mount, a collar to clean up a shifter boot — I model it in OpenSCAD and print it myself. It's become my favorite intersection of problem-solving and making something tangible, which is honestly the same reason I got into software in the first place. This site is a living record of that work — a portfolio, a project log, and proof that the best engineers never really stop tinkering.

This site is where the professional and the personal overlap — because whether it's code, cars, or CAD, tinkering is just what I do.
